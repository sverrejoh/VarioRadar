THE DYNAMIC ISLAND, HONESTLY
What the pill can and cannot do

A black pill that updates when iOS says so, not when you want.

The Dynamic Island feels alive, but it is mostly a series of still frames the system redraws on a tight leash. Here is exactly what renders in it, how often it can change, and which of the internet's "make it update every second" tricks are real versus rejection bait — written for the radar app.

01 · Anatomy

Four presentations, and you do not pick which one shows.

The system chooses the presentation from device state and how many Live Activities are running. You supply all four; iOS decides. Sizes below are approximate render space on a Pro-class device.

Compact · leading

Left of the camera

One glyph or a very short token. Your only "always-on while active" pixels when a single activity runs.

≈ 52–62 pt wide · 37 pt tall
Compact · trailing

Right of the camera

Pairs with leading as one unit. A number, tiny gauge, or our mini approach track.

≈ 52–62 pt wide · 37 pt tall
Minimal

The lone circle

Shown when another app also has an activity. You get a single round glyph; everything else is hidden.

≈ 37 pt circle
Expanded

Long-press / on alert

Four regions (leading, trailing, center, bottom). Briefly auto-shows on an alerting update, then collapses.

up to ~160 pt tall
02 · The render palette

It is a strict subset of SwiftUI. Anything heavier is silently dropped.

The island runs your widget code in a tight, sandboxed extension. Static views render fine. Anything that implies a live process — video, web, maps, scrolling, network image loads — is ignored or shows a placeholder.

▲ Renders
  • ✓Text — styled, custom fonts, Text(timerInterval:), formatted values
  • ✓SF Symbols & bundled images — multicolor/hierarchical, with fade/transition on change
  • ✓Shapes & paths — Capsule, Circle, RoundedRectangle, custom Path
  • ✓Gauge & ProgressView — including the self-filling timerInterval style
  • ✓Colors, gradients, materials, opacity, blend, tint
  • ✓Buttons & Toggles via App Intents (iOS 17+) — tap runs an intent or opens the app
▼ Ignored / unsupported
  • ✕Video — no AVPlayer, no VideoPlayer
  • ✕Maps — Map renders blank; draw your own path instead
  • ✕Animated GIF / APNG — first frame only
  • ✕ScrollView, List, WebView, UIKit (UIViewRepresentable)
  • ✕Network image loading at render time — assets must be bundled or in the App Group
  • ✕Free-form gestures & continuous custom animation (.repeatForever, TimelineView are throttled/dropped)
03 · The refresh truth

The view is a snapshot. Only two things move on their own.

Everything you draw is frozen until the next activity.update() or push. iOS 17+ animates the transition between snapshots (text blurs, numbers roll, symbols fade). But only two primitives keep moving frame-by-frame on the device with no updates at all — because the system drives them off the clock.

Self-interpolatingText/ProgressView(timerInterval:)
smooth, on-device, survives lock & suspend — zero updates needed
Pushed updatesactivity.update() in foreground / BLE wake
discrete snapshots; iOS 17 auto-animates each transition
Suspended pushAPNs, default budget
throttled to a handful/hour; bursts get dropped
Custom animation.repeatForever / TimelineView
unreliable — ignored or throttled once locked/backgrounded
04 · Hard limits

The numbers you cannot argue with.

4 KB
ContentState payload

Max size of one update's state, local or push. Our radar frame is a few hundred bytes — no concern.

8 h
Active lifetime

An activity runs at most 8 hours, then ends. Restart a fresh one for longer rides.

12 h
Lock-screen tail

It can linger on the Lock Screen up to 4 h after ending — but stops updating.

~1 /s
Practical update ceiling

Even with the frequent-updates entitlement, sustained pushes top out around once per second.

05 · The trick ledger

"Update every second in the background" — what's real, what gets you rejected.

The internet is full of ways to keep a Live Activity moving while the app is not foreground. Rated by what survives both iOS and App Review.

01

Self-advancing timer & progress primitives

Drive motion from Text(timerInterval:) / ProgressView(timerInterval:). Apple-sanctioned, runs on-device with zero pushes, keeps moving while locked. The single best trick.

Safe · use it
02

Update from a real background mode you actually use

A genuinely-declared mode that does real work — for us, bluetooth-central: each radar notification wakes the app and we call update() in the callback. Legitimate because the BLE work is real.

Safe · our engine
03

APNs + frequent-updates entitlement

NSSupportsLiveActivitiesFrequentUpdates plus priority-10 pushes, for when the app is fully suspended. Still budget-governed; the user can throttle it in Settings.

Safe · for suspended
04

Alerting update to pop the island + haptic

An update carrying an alert configuration auto-expands the island and buzzes. Perfect for a "fast car" critical event — but spam it and it reads as a broken app.

Safe · sparingly
05

Silent-audio session keep-alive

Play an inaudible track so the app stays alive to run a 1 Hz timer. Works in the simulator, often fails for LA updates on device, and Apple rejects background-audio with no real audio feature.

Rejection bait
06

Background location to stay awake

Defensible only if the app genuinely needs continuous location. For a radar display it does not, and "persistent location with no location feature" is a classic rejection.

Risky · needs justification
06 · The VarioRadar playbook

What all of this means for the radar.

We never need a dirty trick. The radar gives us a legitimate background engine and a use case the safe primitives were built for.

Engine: the BLE wake loop

The radar streams at ~8 Hz. bluetooth-central wakes us on each notification; we call update() at ~1–2 Hz (the island can't usefully show more). Real work, no rejection risk.

Make 1 Hz look fluid

Between updates, let a ProgressView(timerInterval:) closing bar drain and the distance roll with .contentTransition(.numericText()). The car glides instead of teleporting, even if a frame is late.

Compact = the whole story

Bike glyph in leading, nearest distance in trailing. That is the 95% view a rider actually sees. Severity drives the colour; everything legible at a glance.

Escalate, don't nag

Use an alerting update only when a car crosses into "critical" closing — one pop + haptic. Routine traffic stays silent in the compact pill.

Respect the clock

Auto-restart the activity near the 8 h mark for long rides, and keep staleDate short (~5 s) so a dropped connection visibly dims rather than lying.

Degrade honestly

On the Always-On Display the pill renders dimmed at low refresh — design the compact state to read in that muted, slow mode, not just at full brightness.
